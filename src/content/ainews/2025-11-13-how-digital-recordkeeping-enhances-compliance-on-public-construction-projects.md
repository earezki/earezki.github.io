## How Digital Recordkeeping Enhances Compliance on Public Construction Projects

Public construction projects face compliance risks when using paper files or scattered spreadsheets. A 2025 study found that 40% of contractors faced audit delays due to disorganized documentation.

### Why This Matters
Manual systems for tracking labor logs, safety forms, and contracts introduce errors and inefficiencies compared to ideal automated models. Non-compliance can lead to project halts, financial penalties, or loss of future public contracts. For example, missing labor classification records during audits can invalidate entire project certifications, costing contractors millions in lost revenue.

### Key Insights
- "Public projects generate large volumes of records—contracts, labor logs, equipment reports, safety forms, and more."  
- "Digital systems allow teams to access updated plans, daily reports, RFIs, and approvals in real time."  
- "Automated workflows track certifications and schedule safety inspections, reducing compliance errors."

### Practical Applications
- **Use Case**: Government contractors using centralized platforms to verify labor hours and payroll data during audits.  
- **Pitfall**: Relying on manual entry for time tracking, which increases the risk of inconsistent pay rates and compliance violations.
