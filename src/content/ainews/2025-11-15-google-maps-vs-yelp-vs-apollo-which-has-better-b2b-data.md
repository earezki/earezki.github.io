## Google Maps vs Yelp vs Apollo: Which Has Better B2B Data?

A 2025 test scraping 1,000 B2B leads found Google Maps’ phone numbers accurate 92% of the time, while Apollo provided direct emails but faced 15–20% bounce rates.

### Why This Matters
Ideal B2B data would be 100% accurate, complete, and up-to-date, but real-world sources like Google Maps, Yelp, and Apollo each have critical flaws. For example, 8% of Google Maps phone numbers were disconnected, and Apollo’s data was 6–12 months old. Using a single source risks incomplete leads, but combining them—e.g., Google Maps for local reach + Apollo for emails—reduces errors and improves targeting.

### Key Insights
- "Google Maps phone accuracy: 92% (2025 test)"
- "Apollo’s email access but 15–20% bounce rate"
- "Yelp’s 89% phone accuracy but 11% invalid numbers"

### Practical Applications
- **Use Case**: Google Maps for local service providers needing physical location data
- **Pitfall**: Relying on Yelp for B2B leads without filtering out consumer-focused listings
