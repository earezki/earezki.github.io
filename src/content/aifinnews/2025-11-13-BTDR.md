## BTDR – Increase in Days/Weeks

Bitdeer reported a substantial 173.6% year-over-year revenue increase in Q3 2025, but continues to post net losses; however, the recent influx of capital from both convertible note and direct offering sales demonstrates strong investor confidence in their aggressive AI expansion plans.

### Why This Matters
Bitdeer is strategically pivoting from solely a crypto mining solutions provider to a major player in the rapidly expanding AI cloud services sector. This transition, while capital intensive, is crucial for long-term growth and justifies the recent capital raises. The market is currently rewarding companies demonstrating a clear path to leverage the AI boom, and Bitdeer's stated goal of a $2 billion annualized revenue run rate by end of 2026 is a significant catalyst. Concerns around the delay of the SEAL 04 chip are partially offset by their current reliance on established Nvidia technology.

### Key Insights
- **Revenue Growth**: 173.6% year-over-year revenue increase in Q3 2025, reaching $169.7 million.
- **AI Expansion**: Aiming for a $2 billion annualized revenue run rate in AI cloud services by the end of 2026, demonstrating a forward-looking strategy.
- **Capital Raise**: Secured $400 million through convertible notes and $148.4 million through a direct offering, strengthening the balance sheet for expansion.

### Practical Implications
- **Bull Case**: Successful execution of the AI expansion plan, combined with wider adoption of AI cloud services, could drive the stock price towards the upper end of its 52-week high ($27.8) within the next few weeks.
- **Bear Case**: Continued delays with the SEAL 04 chip development or increased competition in the AI cloud market could lead to investor skepticism and a potential price drop towards the lower end of its 52-week range ($6.84).
- **Confidence**: **6/10** – While risks remain (negative free cash flow, high debt), the substantial capital infusion and aggressive AI strategy outweigh them in the short term.
