## FLY – increase in Days/Weeks

Firefly Aerospace reported a Q3 revenue beat of $30.78M versus $27.71M expected, while delivering an adjusted loss of 33 cents per share, better than the anticipated 41 cents. The company also raised its FY2025 revenue guidance to $150–158 million and closed the $855 million SciTech acquisition to enhance software capabilities for the Golden Dome program.

### Why This Matters
Despite negative gross margins and sustained free cash flow burn, Firefly’s improving top-line trajectory, expanding $1.3 billion backlog, and strategic positioning for the $175 billion Golden Dome program signal growing institutional demand and execution credibility—critical momentum drivers in the high-growth aerospace & defense sector where contract visibility translates directly into valuation rerating potential.

### Key Insights
- **Revenue (Q3 2025)**: $30.78M vs. $27.71M estimate, with FY25 guidance raised to $150–158M
- **News Impact**: SciTech acquisition and NASA Blue Ghost/CLIPS awards boost credibility and technical scope
- **Risk/Offset**: Alpha rocket delay and high debt/equity ratio (4.687) increase execution and funding risk

### Practical Implications
- **Bull Case**: Analyst upgrades and raised guidance spark short-covering rally; stock retests 52-week high of $73.80
- **Bear Case**: Continued negative margins and launch delays trigger profit-taking; pullback to $18–20 range
- **Confidence**: **7/10** – Earnings momentum and strategic moves outweigh near-term risks
