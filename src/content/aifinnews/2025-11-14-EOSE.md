## EOSE – increase in Days/Weeks

Eos Energy Enterprises reported a negative gross margin of -177.94% and free cash flow of -$187 million, highlighting ongoing operational losses. However, the company just reported its highest quarterly revenue of $30.5 million in Q3 2025—doubling the prior quarter—and secured a 228 MWh order from Frontier Power under a 5 GWh framework agreement.

### Why This Matters
Despite deep profitability challenges, Eos is transitioning from a pre-revenue stage to meaningful commercial scale, with revenue nearly doubling full-year 2024 levels in a single quarter and a $586.8 million backlog providing visibility. The recent manufacturing expansion in Pennsylvania, backed by $24 million in state incentives, enhances credibility in execution, while strategic interest from Cerberus Capital and rising institutional ownership signal growing confidence in its Znyth battery technology—particularly for AI-driven power demand—making this a pivotal growth inflection point.

### Key Insights
- **Revenue Growth**: $30.5 million in Q3 2025, doubling Q2 and nearing full-year 2024 total
- **News Impact**: 228 MWh Frontier Power order + Cerberus LOI for 960 MWh → near-term revenue visibility
- **Risk/Offset**: Gross margin at -177.94% and negative FCF → dilution risk if funding new growth

### Practical Implications
- **Bull Case**: Continued order flow and commercial execution → 15–20% upside in 4 weeks
- **Bear Case**: High cash burn and negative margins → potential 10–15% pullback if financing news emerges
- **Confidence**: **7/10** – Strong revenue acceleration and institutional backing outweigh near-term fundamentals
