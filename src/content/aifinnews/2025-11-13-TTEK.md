## TTEK – Increase in Days/Weeks

Tetra Tech reported a significant earnings beat in Q3 2025 with revenue up 7.7% YoY and EPS exceeding estimates by 18.5%, while simultaneously issuing promising FY26 guidance for substantial revenue and EPS growth. Despite a recent three-month dip, boosted by prior earnings releases, the undervaluation suggests a potential reversal.

### Why This Matters
The current market environment prioritizes companies demonstrating both revenue growth and improving profitability, and Tetra Tech delivers on both fronts. While the stock has experienced some pullback related to broader technical corrections, the recent strong performance and forward-looking guidance signal resilience and potential for upward momentum as investor confidence returns, particularly within the increasingly important environmental engineering and sustainable infrastructure sectors. This becomes particularly relevant as infrastructure spending increases globally.

### Key Insights
- **Q3 2025 Revenue**: $1.23 billion, exceeding expectations by 17.3%.
- **FY26 EPS Guidance**: Projected EPS of $1.48, beating estimates by 4.1%.
- **Debt/Equity**: 61.514 which is high, but the increased profitability can offset this risk.

### Practical Implications
- **Bull Case**: Positive analyst sentiment (3 Buy, 2 Strong Buy) combined with the undervalued fair value estimate of $42.60 could drive the stock towards this target within the next few weeks, representing a potential 25% increase.
- **Bear Case**: Continued broader market volatility or concerns regarding the debt level could limit gains and potentially lead to a 5-10% retracement in the short term.
- **Confidence**: **7/10** – The strong recent earnings and guidance are offset by the current market environment and existing debt concerns.
